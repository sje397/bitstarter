Weader is a project designed to engage children ages four to eight in the process of learning to read. Using a smart phone or tablet, children can read stories assisted by advanced speech recognition and synthesis technologies. Accompanied by interactive illustration, dynamic text and easy to access tools, children can easily develop their reading skills in a fun and encouraging environment. Older children can develop their own stories and illustrations, as well share and collaborate with friends. This site has been set up to fund the Weader project. To get it off the ground, we need to fund software development, staff salaries, consultations with educators and developmental psychologists, as well as the setup of business and legal structures.

If you've watched a four-year-old handle a modern mobile device, you'll know that children of the next generation are digital natives. Their fluency with technology is the perfect tool to help during this critical age as they learn to read. The Weader App will allow children to choose stories that interest them, to watch interactive illustrations as they read along, while the app tracks their progress. They can tap to hear a difficult word spoken out loud, or sounded out phonetically.

Who are you?
My name is Scott Ellis. I'm an experienced software engineer who has worked professionally on automation systems in the mining industry for more than a decade. I also develop for the web and have a keen interest in machine learning. I am the father of three awesome young beta-testers children, two of which are still learning to read.
What technologies are involved?
OpenEars is a speech recognition and synthesis toolkit for iOS, the operating system used on the iPhone and iPad, and will be at the heart of the Weader app. Similar technologies are available for other platforms.
What is the timeframe for development?
I have already begun. Depending on funding, I hope to have the app out in beta by the end of 2013.
